Childhood and Family

Mark Anthony Miloslavic Jr. was born in Fremont, California on October 25, 1984, the first American-born child of immigrant parents, Mark Sr (Peruvian) and Susana (Venezuelan). He is the oldest of four siblings: two brothers, Miguel and Santiago, and two sisters, Stephanie and Jessica.

All throughout high school, Mark was enrolled as a cadet in the JROTC program. Some of his favorite hobbies as a teenager include web designing, making Adobe Flash animations, and photo editing with Adobe Photoshop. In 2003, he graduated from Paul R. Wharton high school (go wildcats!) and quickly enlisted in the U.S. Marine Corps.

Military and Fatherhood

Throughout his Marine Corps career, Mark was stationed in Japan and southern California. During his time in Japan, he was deployed to South Korea, Thailand, and the Philippines for training exercises and humanitarian aid. In 2006, Mark was deployed to a war-torn Iraq in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom. On December 2011, after eight and a half years of active military service, he was honorably discharged and relocated to North Carolina.

Mark is a loving father, blessed with two beautiful and healthy little girls, Kaylee and Olivia. They all enjoy doing fun and exciting activities together like visiting local parks, aquariums and zoos, swimming and making sandcastles at the beach, exploring art and science museums, painting creative and pretty canvases, playing board games, and making silly Snapchat videos everywhere they go.

Education

In 2012, Mark enrolled into Coastal Carolina Community College and began his journey as a college student. Mark always had a fascination with science, technology, engineering and mathematics, so he decided to pursue an education in biology. In 2018, he was awarded a Bachelor of Science in Biology from the University of North Carolina at Wilmington. That same year, he quickly moved to UNC Charlotte and completed the Graduate Certification in Bioinformatics Technology program.
